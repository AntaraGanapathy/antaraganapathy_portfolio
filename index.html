About Me

I am Antara Ganapathy, a student from Bangalore, India, with a deep passion for computer science, specifically AI/ML. My interest in this field stems from my fascination with the capability of technology to solve daily struggles efficiently and effectively. My other interests include Bharatanatyam (an Indian classical dance form), playing the guitar, and entrepreneurship. I have been learning Bharatanatyam since 2013 and gave my Arangetram, a dancer’s first solo performance, in 2016 (a picture of me performing at my Arangetram on the left).

Recently, I have been exploring my interest in computer vision and have taken up several projects on the subject. In addition, I recently wrote a research paper that focused on the intersection between technology and healthcare, specifically the application of computer vision in medicine, which has been accepted by the Young Scientist Journal by Vanderbilt University. Furthermore, I recently combined my entrepreneurial experience and tech skills to start my own venture: Budgetary, a money and task management app for university students.

Projects

Python AI/ML Computer Vision
Pneumonia Detection
According to the WHO, pneumonia is the single largest infectious cause of death for children under the age of 5, with approximately one child dying every 39 seconds, as per UNICEF data. Hence, it is imperative to determine an efficient way to diagnose this medical condition. Through this project, I applied my understanding of computer vision to train an ML model to detect pneumonia in children under the age of 5.
